Critically Endangered

The critically endangered conservation status is a species that faces high risks of future extinction. The critically endangered species would have huge factors to worry about since they are at very high risk. With an individual’s help, they could be out of the critically endangered conservation status and well-treated. To help today, check out the page Prevention. It talks about how to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help. There are currently 9,065 critically endangered species of fungi, animals, plants, and chromist. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species.

Fungi

Fungi in critically endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease, There are currently 32 critically endangered species of fungi. Some critically endangered fungi include Boreal Felt lichen, Peruvian Orange-bush Lichen, barbie Pagoda, Starry Breck Lichen, Smooth Horsehair Lichen, Yellow-gilled Lepiota, and Hongo de Coco.

Plants

Plants in critically endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease, Some critically endangered plants include Middelburg Cycad, Palmita, Siamese Rosewood, Palma Corcho, Kraaifontein Spiderhead, Venda Cycad, and Wollemi Pine.

Animals

Animals in critically endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, and poaching. There are currently 3,797 critically endangered speciesof animals. Some critically endangered animals include Javan Rhinoceros, Angel shark, Blue-throated Macaw, Hooded Vulture, Great Hammerhead, Bleeding Toad, Siamese Crocodile, Black Rhino, and California Condor.

Chromist

Chromista in critically endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently four critically endangered species of chromist. Some critically endangered chromist include Dictyota galapagensis, Desmarestia tropica, Spatoglossum schmittii, and Bifurcaria galapagensis.
