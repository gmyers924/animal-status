Vulnerable

The vulnerable conservation status is a species that is threatened with extinction unless the situation that is threatening the species improves. Vulnerable species would have some factors to worry about, so an individual should try to find a way to stop the extinction and threat. With an individual’s help, they could be out of the vulnerable conservation status and well-treated. To help today, check out the page Prevention. It talks about how to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help. There are currently 16,300 vulnerable species of fungi, animals, and plants. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species.

Fungi

Fungi in vulnerable conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena , genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 151 vulnerable species of fungi. Some fungi vulnerable include Chinese Caterpillar Fungus, Date Waxcap, bittermusseron, Evelyn's Buttons, Rock Gnome Lichen, and Violet Coral.

Plants

Plants in vulnerable conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena , genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 9,221 vulnerable species of plants. Some vulnerable plants include Sapele, Clover Grass, Argun, Chatham Island Christmas tree, Big Leaf Mahogany, Species code: Zs, and Mt.

Animals

Animals vulnerable conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena , genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, and poaching. There are currently 6,927 vulnerable species of animals. Some vulnerable animals include Maned Three-toed Sloth , Spectacled Bear, Sun Bear, Cheetah, Andean Flamingo, Cuban Greater Funnel-eared Bat, Polar Bear, Giant Anteater, and Giant Armadillo.

Chromistan

Chromista vulnerable conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena , genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There is currently one vulnerable species of chromist. The vulnerable chromist is Eisenia galapagensis.
