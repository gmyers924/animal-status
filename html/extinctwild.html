Extinction In the Wild

The extinct wild conservation status is when all the species in the wild have gone extinct. The species extinct in the wild would have low expedition for survival since there are only some left and there are none out in the wild, but they could be reintroduced into the wild. To help today, check out the page Prevention. It talks about what is being done to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help. There are currently 82 species of animals and plants extinct in the wild. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species.

Plants

Plants are extinct in the wild conservation status due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 44 species of plants extinct in the wild. Some plants extinct in the wild include Franklin Tree, St Helena Redwood, She Cabbage Tree, Governor Laffan's Fern, Kalimantan Mango, Escarpment Cycad, Brome des Ardennes, Fuzzyflower Cyrtandra, and Blue Cycad.

Animals

Animals are extinct in the wild conservation status due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, and poaching. There are currently 38 species of animals extinct in the wild. Some animals extinct in the wild include Scimitar-horned Oryx, Wyoming Toad, Christmas Island Chained Gecko, Alagoas Curassow, Socorro Dove, Père David's Deer, Captain Cook's Bean Snail, Hawaiian Crow, and Spix's Macaw.
