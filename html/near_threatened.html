Near Threatened

The near-threatened conservation status is a species that could be prone to endangerment soon. Near-threatened species would not have many risk factors to worry about, but they are still a factor to worry about a little. With an individual’s help, they could be out of the near-threatened conservation status and well-treated. To help today, check out the page Prevention. It talks about how to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help. There are currently 8,714 near-threatened species of fungi, animals, and plants. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species.

Fungi

Fungi in near-threatened conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, poaching, and mass extinctions. There are currently 61 near-threatened species of fungi. Some fungi near-threatened include Imperial Mushroom, Sand Deceiver, Marsh Honey Fungus, Witches Cauldron, Wrinkled Peach, Slimy Earthtongue, and Ruddy Bolete.

Plants

Plants in near-threatened conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, poaching, and mass extinctions. There are 3,668near threatened. Some plants near-threatened include Roe's Cypress-pine, Kitsaoso, Verdon Spleenwort, Woolly Tree Fern, Irish Pouncewort, Juniper Prongwort, and Philippine Paphiopedilum.

Animals

Animals in near-threatened conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, poaching, and mass extinctions. There are currently 4,985 near-threatened species of animals. Some animals near-threatened include the Spiny-headed Tree Frog, Mexican Blind Brotula, Gypsum Sand Grasshopper, Hasenpusch Family Stick-insect, Amami Takachiho Snake, Iwasaki's Snail-eater, Pacu, Hoary Fox, and Maned Wolf.
