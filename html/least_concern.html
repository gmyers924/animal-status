Least Concern

The least concerned conservation status is a species that is not focused on because its population is big enough not to worry about. The least-concerned species would not have risk factors to worry about. To help today with species beyond conservation status, check out the page Prevention. It talks about how to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help. There are currently 75,733 least concerned species of fungi, animals, and plants. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species.

Fungi

Fungi least concerned about conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 201 least concerned species of fungi. Some fungi least concerned include Strathy Strangler, Bearded Tooth, Caesar's Amanita, Amanita caesarea, Bleeding Blue Tooth, Umbilicaria phaea, and Grönkremla.

Plants least concerned about conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. Some plants least concerned include Fuca n’ Lepo, Bush Vetch, Hungarian Vetch, Japanese Nutmeg Tree, Alpine Rose, Orange Foxtail, Giant Reed, and Arundo donax.

Animals

Animals least concerned about conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, and poaching. There are currently 47,528 least concerned species of animals. Some animals least concerned include American Snout, Asian Snake-eyed Skink, Striped Seabream, Seychelles Stick Insect, Oman Rock Gecko, Sharpsnout Seabream, Long-nosed Tree Snake, and African Flap-necked Chameleon.
