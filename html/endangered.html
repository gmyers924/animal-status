Endangered

The endangered conservation status is a species that is likely to go extinct. Endangered species would have a bunch of factors to worry about, so individuals should try to find a way to stop the extinction. With an individual’s help, they could be out of the endangered conservation status and well-treated. To help today, check out the page Prevention. It talks about how to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help. There are currently 16,094 endangered species of fungi, animals, plants, and chromist. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species.

Fungi

Fungi in endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 101 endangered species of fungi. Some endangered fungi include Green Cage Fungus, Arctic Orange Bush Lichen, Mid-Atlantic Comb-over, Golden Spruce Dots, Old Gray Crosslobes, Woven-spore Lichen, and Tammy's Pumpkin Pails.

Plants

Plants in endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 9,996 endangered species of plants. Some endangered plants include Korean Fir, Waterwheel, Palma Real, Asian Surf Grass, and Queen of the Andes.

Animals

Animals in endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, and poaching. There are currently 5,996 endangered species of animals. Some endangered animals include Antsingy Leaf Chameleon, Tiger, Belearic Lizard, Aye-aye, Marine Otter, Giant Slippery Frog, Proboscis Monkey, and Hog Deer.

Chromistan

Chromista in endangered conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There is currently one endangered species of chromist. The endangered chromist is Sargassum setifolium.
