Extinction

Extinction is when a species is deceased completely. About 99% of the 4 billion species went extinct throughout the years. Some species have gone extinct due to human hunting, factories causing pollution, and the destruction of habitats. There are currently 902 extinct species of animals and plants. More knowledge of the situation could help, so individuals spreads the word around to help end the suffering of different species. To help today and prevent this from happening in the future, check out the page Prevention. It talks about how to prevent species from going extinct and ways individuals can help.

Plants

Plants in extinct conservation status are due to genetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, and disease. There are currently 124 extinct species of plants. Some extinct plants include †Galápagos Amaranth, †St Helena Ebony Tree, †Pepino de Galápagos, †Adams Mistletoe, †Waitakere Scurvy Grass, and †St Helena Olive.

Animals

Animals in extinct conservation status are due togenetics, demographic phenomena, genetic pollution, habitat degradation, predation, competition, disease, and poaching. There are currently 778 extinct species of animals. Some extinct animals include the †Saber-toothed cat, †Dodo, †Woolly Mammoth, †Ground Sloth, †Thylacines, †Trilobites, and †Golden Toad.
